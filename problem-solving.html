BEING BLOCKED

I got blocked quite a few times, don't know if I should call them 'simple problems' as they were like the Alps in my head

One of them is especially hard for me, I'm saying about you map(). When I was dealing with .map(), god knows how much I miss my simple old friend for loops. I almost used all the problem-solving techniques on the list plus a lot of taking deep breath to help calm myself down and not thinking about smash my laptop. When I finally did it, it felt pretty good, and the code looks really simple just a few line of code. Just when I was about to relax, the second day, I got stuck on .map() again when doing another kata. Well, guess we are not friends yet. I learned that sometimes when I have successfully used the method once, it does not mean that I have mastered it, there is no end to learning.

PROBLEM SOLVING TECHNIQUES


Pseudocode:

I'm still trying to get into the habit. The kata I've encountered so far are relatively small and simple ones, and I feel that the code is pretty self-explanatory.

Trying Something:

I use this technique the most, and in most cases I alter it here and there to see what comes up, although in most cases it's the red error message, well, worth a try though.

Rubber Ducky Method:

I really like the principle of this technique, but like the Pseudocode technique, since the tasks I've encountered so far are relatively simple, I normally get stuck on syntax problems rather than logic. So, I haven’t use this method much yet, but I think it won’t be far for me to have to rely on this method a lot.

Reading Error Messages:

I like this one! It helps me to at least identify where I should be looking at.

Console.logging:

Console.log is my good friend!

Googling:

When I try to solve kata I often use Google, sometimes I search and read in English and sometimes in Chinese. There is a wealth of information in both languages for my problem, and quite often the information in both languages can do a pretty good job in making me confused.

Asking Your Peers for Help:

Not very confident yet, but I guess it will get better when we move to face to face Bootcamp.

Asking Coaches for Help:

Feeling least confident in this one as I’ve never been the student that raise their hands up and ask questions in class. Wish someday I’ll get better in this.
